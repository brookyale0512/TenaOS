# TenaOS Applied System Card

## System

TenaOS is a local-first clinical AI operating system for primary-care workflows. It combines OpenMRS, Gemma 4 E4B, a WHO/MSF guideline knowledge base, a CIEL terminology knowledge base, deterministic middleware, and clinician review.

## Intended Use

TenaOS is intended to help clinics configure and operate standards-based digital health workflows:

- draft OpenMRS forms from natural language,
- structure clinician notes into SOAP sections and coded observations,
- generate guideline-grounded clinical decision support cards,
- draft patient education materials,
- translate plain-language reporting questions into deterministic FHIR query plans,
- support CIEL-backed clinical data capture and reporting.

TenaOS is designed for clinician-in-the-loop operation where clinical artifacts remain reviewable before use.

## Intended Users

- Clinical officers, nurses, and facility staff reviewing AI-generated artifacts.
- Facility managers building reports from OpenMRS data.
- Technical implementers deploying and maintaining the local stack.
- Researchers evaluating local-first clinical AI workflows.

## Input Data

- Natural-language form/report requests.
- Text or voice clinical notes.
- OpenMRS patient context, encounters, observations, vitals, and notes.
- Local WHO/MSF guideline chunks.
- Local CIEL concept bundles and semantic search candidates.

## Outputs

- Draft OpenMRS forms and schemas.
- SOAP notes and structured extraction candidates.
- Cited CDS cards.
- Patient-facing education materials.
- FHIR query plans and report results.
- Audit traces and draft histories.

## Model and Retrieval Components

- Generation model: Gemma 4 E4B served locally through `llama.cpp`.
- Guideline retrieval: EmbedGemma 300M dense vectors plus BM25 sparse vectors in Qdrant.
- CIEL semantic retrieval: SapBERT dense vectors plus BM25 sparse vectors in Qdrant.
- CIEL authoritative validation: local SQLite/FTS5 concept bundle store.

## Human Oversight

TenaOS is designed for human-in-the-loop use:

- AI-generated forms remain drafts until reviewed and published.
- Scribe extractions are reviewed before chart entry.
- CDS and patient education are informational drafts for clinician review.
- Reports are generated by deterministic query plans and interpreted by staff familiar with local data quality.

## Safety Controls

- Tool calls are allow-listed.
- The model writes through validated drafts and OpenMRS middleware.
- CIEL concepts are validated locally before use.
- Retired concepts are rejected by default.
- Datatype and concept-class checks prevent invalid form/report fields.
- Report plans are compiled deterministically before FHIR execution.
- CDS and patient education cite retrieved evidence.
- Unsupported answers use abstention behavior rather than invented recommendations.
- Runtime traces are persisted or streamed for auditability.

## Privacy and Data Boundary

TenaOS is designed to run locally. OpenMRS, MariaDB, Qdrant, Gemma inference, CIEL, and workflow traces can run inside the local container stack. The public demonstration environment uses synthetic data.

Production operators configure `TENAOS_PROFILE=production`, rotate credentials, terminate public traffic over HTTPS, secure service accounts, and comply with local regulatory requirements.

## Operational Controls

- CIEL mapping is controlled through local concept validation, datatype checks, and clinician review.
- Guideline retrieval is controlled through citation grounding, evidence traces, and clinician review.
- Synthetic and teacher-generated data are tracked as adaptation data with validation gates and provenance.
- Audio and multilingual behavior use the same reviewable extraction and confirmation workflow.
- Reports are interpreted in the context of local OpenMRS data quality.
- Local deployment uses explicit GPU, memory, storage, and artifact requirements.

## Current Evidence

- CIEL SQLite: 58,687 concepts, 298,905 mappings, 8,545 Q-and-A edges, 3,259 concept-set edges.
- WHO/MSF guideline KB: 69,476 chunks across 401 chunk files.
- LoRA/SFT corpus: 16,005 validated task-tagged traces split into 18,909 training, 1,071 validation, and 1,109 held-out test conversations.
- Internal technical evaluation form-builder baseline: 147/147 completed, 0 failures, 0.465 mean recall, 0.993 schema-valid rate, 17.97 s median latency.

These artifacts document the completed implementation, local knowledge assets, and internal technical evaluation package.

## Deployment Governance

- Site validation in representative settings.
- Local data protection and security review.
- Workflow-specific safety review by clinicians.
- Bias and subgroup review where relevant.
- Deployment monitoring for drift, failures, and unsafe outputs.
- Incident response and rollback procedures.
- Governance process for prompt, model, KB, and terminology updates.

## Versioning and Monitoring

Recommended release metadata:

- TenaOS git commit.
- Gemma model artifact name and checksum.
- EmbedGemma and SapBERT artifact versions.
- CIEL source version and SQLite checksum.
- WHO/MSF KB snapshot checksum.
- Prompt hash set and optimized prompt overlay version.
- Evaluation summary and operating boundaries for the release.
